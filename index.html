The Problem

A $40B trade
flying blind

Cut flowers are the world's most time-sensitive commodity. Yet the industry still routes perishable cargo on guesswork, manual paperwork, and the farmer's gut feeling.

01

Zero Shelf Life Visibility

Flowers move through 6+ hands — grower, packer, freight forwarder, wholesaler, distributor, retailer — with no shared data on remaining vase life. A Nairobi rose has no idea what Amsterdam warehouse temperatures did to its clock.

→ $15B+ in annual post-harvest losses
02

Compliance as a Blocker

Phytosanitary certificates, ISPM-15 compliance, country-specific pesticide thresholds, and import restrictions differ across every border. A single non-conformance can ground a full shipment within 72 hours of harvest.

→ Rejected shipments cost $8–40K each
03

Demand & Supply Misalignment

Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, Diwali — demand spikes are predictable, yet overstock and stockouts plague every major event. Buyers can't find the right growers. Growers can't find qualified buyers. The matching layer is WhatsApp.

→ 22% average perishable inventory waste

How It Works

From farm to
forecast in minutes

Klairen ingests supply chain data at every node and returns actionable intelligence — not dashboards you have to interpret.

01

Ingest

Connect via API, IoT sensor feed, or manual entry. Temperature logs, variety data, harvest timestamps, and logistics events flow into the Klairen data layer.

02

Predict

The shelf life engine computes remaining vase life per batch using time-temperature integration and variety-specific decay models — recalculated at every leg of the journey.

03

Comply

Automated compliance checks against destination-country phytosanitary requirements. Flags pesticide threshold violations and generates certificate documentation before border crossing.

04

Act

Route deviation alerts, demand-matched reallocation suggestions, and buyer introductions — delivered to the right person, at the right node, before the window closes.
